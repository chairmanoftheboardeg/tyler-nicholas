The Digital Disconnect: A Critique of Government Communication

Why establish digital communication channels if they function as black holes? A look into the unresponsiveness of government departments, specifically DICT Seychelles.

As we push toward a more digitized and efficient economy in Seychelles, the expectations for government communication naturally evolve. Yet, there remains a glaring inefficiency in how state departments handle public inquiries. The most concerning example is the Department of Information Communications Technology (DICT).

For a department explicitly tasked with managing "Information" and "Communications," it is profoundly ironic that sending an email to their official contact addresses often results in absolute silence. What is the purpose of maintaining public-facing email addresses or digital contact forms if the internal protocol to actually read and respond to them does not seem to exist?

As an entrepreneur managing digital ventures like BlueWave Digital, I know firsthand that systemic precision is non-negotiable. Whether you are operating the logistics of a virtual aviation group or managing a national IT infrastructure, a broken communication loop is a critical failure. If we want to foster a true digital economy, our government departments must move beyond simply having a web presence and commit to actual, responsive digital governance.
